Free Books on the Asus!

Don't get me wrong. It's not that books (especially free ones) aren't cool. In fact, they are so cool, they deserve their own page.

Google Books

There are several ways to read books and magazines for free on an Android device. One way is to download and install Google Books from the Google Play store. Once you install the app, you can open it and browse for free books by, for example, selecting “TOP FREE” as shown in this screen cap. Each book is represented by a cover image, the title, author, and a star rating.

Wikipedia indicates that a Google Play book "is only a license to read the book. If the purchaser happens to travel to a country where Google Play Books are not sold then the books on the device can be deleted. In such a case, the books would have to be re-downloaded after the traveler returns to a country where they are sold."

Building Your Library

Adding books couldn't be more intuitive. You simply tap your selection and you get a screen with additional details such as the publisher, original date of publication, and number of pages.

A synopsis and reader reviews can help you decide whether you want to add the book. If you do, you just tap "ADD TO LIBRARY" and it's fait accompli!

Of course, like a lot of comments on the internet, many reviews will be of little or no value, and you will have to wade through the flotsam to find treasures.

The Reading Experience

The Google Books reader experience is much like reading on a Kindle.

The video Google Play Books for Android Review by The eBook Reader walks through the available features:

  • Adjusting brightness
  • Normal, Sepia, and Night themes (white on black)
  • Changing the font properties (size, line height, style)
  • Justifying left or center
  • Text-to-speech (“Read Aloud”)
  • Bookmarking
  • Highlighting
  • Dictionary
  • Translation
  • Map popup for places
  • Table of Contents
  • Note-taking
  • Viewing original scanned pages
