Android Tablets

In class, we checked out the Asus Memo Pad (ASUS ME173x).

My tablet had the following features:

ProcessorQuad Core 1.2 GHz
Storage11.82 GB
RAM1 GB
OSAndroid 4.2.1
Battery Life10 hours
Weight10.7 ounces

*This tablet costs around $170 on amazon.com, but it can be found for as low as $129.

Android 4.2.1 Customizations

This page was added to the Android Settings by Asus, the company that makes this tablet. Here you can tweak various options, such as screenshots, gamer options, power options, scrolling options, and Asus specific hardware such as the trans cover. To see a full sized image, right click the image and choose View Image or Open image in new tab.

The Android 4.2.1 Apps Screen

The Apps Screen is a scrollable area that displays the apps installed on the device and allows you to launch them with a single tap. This is like having shortcuts to all of your applications hanging around your Windows Desktop and changing your Windows settings to launch them with a single click. (By default, they appear in ascending alpha order by app name but by the looks of it, you can change that.)

Viewing Storage Information

The Storage screen is accessible from Settings. Here you can see the type of storage you are using and how it is being allocated. Color coding helps you to manage your storage. For example, if you see a huge swatch of purple hogging that gray bar, you are using a lot storage for video and photos. It's probably time to push some of that junk out to Google Drive or somewhere else in the cloud.
